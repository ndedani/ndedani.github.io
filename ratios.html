Ratio Type Ratio Description
Current Ratio 1.46
Current Assets/ Current Liabilities 		Relationship of current assets to current Liabilities. Good current ratio is between 1.5 and 2.
Quick Ratio
(Acid Test Ratio)		 1.06:1
[(Cash + Temp. Investments + Accounts Receivable) / Current Liabilities]: 1 		The relationship between the amount of assets that can quickly be turned into cash versus the amount of current liabilities. A quick ratio of 1 or above is considered good.
Debt to Equity 2.2:1
(Total liabilities / Total Stockholders' Equity) : 1 		Proportion of a company's assets supplied by the company's creditors versus the amount supplied by the owner or stockholders. Debt-to-Equity ratio of around 2 or 2.5 is generally considered good.
Debt to total assets
(Acid Test Ratio)		 0.62:1
(Total liabilities / Total Assets) : 1 		The proportion of a company's assets that was provided b creditors. Debt-to-Total Assets ratio of 0.3 to 0.6 is preferred. Higher the ratio, more it is said to be leveraged.
Gross Margin
(Acid Test Ratio)		 43.0%
Gross Profit / Net Sales 		percentage of sales dollars available for expenses and profit after the cost of merchandise is deducted from sales. Margin of 50 to 70% is considered good.
Profit Margin
(Acid Test Ratio)		 24%
Net Income after Tax / Net Sales 		Profit per sales dollar after all expenses are deducted from sales. Profit margin of 50 to 70% is considered healthy.
